# Research PPT Figure

## When to use
- Turn dense research content into slide-ready visual explanations.
- Create conceptual diagrams, method pipelines, teaser figures, or presentation cover graphics.
- Improve slide visuals that feel crowded, text-heavy, or visually flat.
- Bridge diagram drafts from `$mermaid-diagram` to polished visuals via `$paper-illustration`.

## Workflow
1. Reduce the slide to one core message.
   If the audience cannot say the takeaway in one sentence, the slide is still too busy.
2. Build hierarchy before decoration.
   Start from headline, focal figure, and supporting labels; only then add arrows, badges, or background treatments.
3. Use large shapes and obvious grouping.
   Research talks are viewed from a distance, so weak hierarchy disappears quickly.
4. Prefer clean geometric structure over ornamental complexity.
   Use whitespace, alignment, contrast, and repetition to create polish.
5. Prepare assets for reuse.
   Export diagrams as SVG or high-resolution PNG when they may be reused in papers or posters.

## Quality bar
- A viewer should understand the slide's point within a few seconds.
- Text density should be low enough for live presentation, not just screen reading.
- Visual emphasis should land on the key result, method step, or comparison.
- Colors should support grouping and emphasis instead of turning every object into an accent.
- Final composition should still work in a standard 16:9 slide layout.
